# Aurora Release Poster Design

## Purpose

Create one 3:5 vertical release poster for `luci-theme-aurora`. The poster should be recognizable as Aurora without reproducing a complete LuCI interface or becoming a conventional product advertisement.

## Source Visual Language

- Light mode supplies the dominant surface: a cold near-white derived from `--bg: oklch(0.984 0.004 235)`.
- Dark mode supplies the chromatic anchor: the cyan-green brand token `--brand: oklch(0.6 0.13 188.745)`.
- Aurora's logo contributes its folded, translucent aurora-strip silhouette and a restrained cyan-green to violet-gray transition.
- Rounded cards, thin hairlines, soft shadows, translucent overlays, and mono microcopy translate the interface system into paper artifacts.

## Approved Direction

**Aurora through the router**: a small folded aurora strip floats below center on a cold-white, aged paper field. Faint network topology and fragmentary LuCI panel geometry appear inside or immediately around the strip, suggesting a router interface without depicting a literal dashboard.

The image cluster occupies about 18% of the canvas and the paper field about 82%. The high-chroma cyan-green area occupies about 1.5%–2.5% of the full canvas and remains visible at thumbnail size. A slight violet-gray edge may appear only as part of the logo-derived transition, not as a competing accent.

## Typography and Copy

- Primary phrase: `QUIETLY CONNECTED.`
- Secondary line: `为 OpenWrt 留一束安静的光`
- Archive microcopy: `LUCI THEME / AURORA / 2026`
- Use sparse typewriter or monospaced typography with minor misregistration and imperfect ink transfer. Keep the primary phrase readable; allow microcopy to remain semi-legible.

## Material and Mood

Use matte absorbent paper, visible fibers, risograph grain, light xerox wear, gentle ink bleed, and a flat orthographic scan. The emotional temperature is quiet, precise, slightly nostalgic, and technically lucid.

## Constraints

- No full-bleed scene, literal stock router, complete UI screenshot, logo lockup, CTA, feature list, badges, or commercial headline hierarchy.
- No glossy mockup, hard shadow, cinematic lighting, depth of field, 3D rendering, neon, cyberpunk, anime, or dense scrapbook treatment.
- Preserve one dominant high-chroma hue and 70%–90% negative space.

## Deliverables and Acceptance

Deliver one generated raster poster and the exact four-paragraph Standard Mode prompt. Accept when the poster reads as sparse paper at thumbnail scale, the cyan-green anchor is unmistakable, the cluster remains compact, the release context is legible, and the overall image feels native to both Aurora and Minimal Zine Poster v0.1.
